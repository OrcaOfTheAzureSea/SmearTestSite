#StopSmearFear

Have you got worries around smear test?

What is a smear test?

A smear test, which is also known as a cervical screening test, is a screening that checks for cervical cancer by collecting cells from the cervix. This detects any abnormal changes and helps decide if these changes should need treated. The NHS offers regular cervical screening for woman aged 25 to 64 who are registered with a GP, who have a cervix and have ever been sexually active. Its an important step in taking care of your health! A smear test also includes testing for HPV (human papillomavirus). HPV is a common virus that can lead to cervical cancer. By detecting the presence of HPV, smear tests can help identify woman who may be at higher risk of developing cervical cancer

The Smear Test Process

During your smear test,you will be asked to lie down on the examination bed. A healthcare professional will then gently insert a speculum into the vagina to get a clear view of the cervix They will then use a small brush or spatula to collect a sample of cells from the cervix. This sample is then sent to a lab for analysis. If have questions or worries about the test, let the nurse or doctor know. They will understand and try to make you comfortable.

How Long Will It Take

A smear test is a quick and easy process which typically lasts around 5 minutes. It should not hurt, but sometimes it can be uncomfortable

How Often should you get one

It is recommended that you should have a smear test every 3 years or every 5 years if you are aged over 50.

What can i do

There are many reasons why woman avoid smear tests, but it is important to remember that smear tests are a crucial part of preventive healthcare.
Fear and Anxiety
Shame or lack of awareness
Embarrassment or discomfort
Some woman may feel anxious or uncomfortable about the procedure. Others may feel shame or embarrassed. Some woman lack awareness, whereas others may just simply feel fear. These feelings are normal, and you are not alone.

How to overcome these barriers

  • Time your appointment with your period. Find a time when you don’t have your period
  • Talk with your nurse or doctor before your appointment
  • >Wear comfortable clothing. This will help you feel more relaxed.
  • You can request a female to do the test when you are making the appointment
  • Bring someone you trust with you to your appointment
  • Relax your muscles to make testing easier
  • Ask to change position if your uncomfortable

Receiving your results

You’ll receive the results of your smear test via letter. When you’re at your cervical screening appointment, your nurse or doctor can tell you how long this is likely to take. Try not to worry about your results, even if they are later than you expect. Most people have a normal result. The letter will explain what they tested and what they mean. If the HPV virus is not found in your sample, your risk of cervical cancer is very low. If the HPV virus is found, you’ll be told what you need to do next. This could involve a repeat test in a year or another screening test called a colposcopy. This is like a smear test but is carried out at a hospital.

Empowering Stories

I was 30 years old when I received my first smear test. I was terrified, not necessarily about the pain but how embarrassed I would feel. Only when I became worried about some abnormal bleeding did I really consider booking my smear test. I was glad I did, as it turned out I had some abnormal cells that needed treated. The procedure itself was not how I imagined it to be. I felt comfortable relaxed. - Anonymous..
